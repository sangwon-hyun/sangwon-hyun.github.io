*** Trisha Dawn (Incoming)

#+ATTR_HTML: :width 40% 
[[../../img/trisha-pic.jpeg]]

I am a final-year doctoral candidate in the Department of Statistics at Texas
A&M University, where I am jointly advised by Yang Ni and Jesús Arroyo. My
research interests broadly lie at the intersection of novel methodological
development and real-world applications, with a focus on causal discovery, graph
matching, and change-point detection. Specifically, my current research includes
Bayesian non-parametrics, optimization, machine learning, and high-dimensional
statistical inference for addressing problems arising in spatial
transcriptomics, single-cell multi-omics, alignment of network-valued data, and
high-dimensional or network-valued time-dependent observations. I completed my
Masters and Bachelors in Statistics from Indian Statistical Institute and
St. Xavier’s College (Autonomous), Kolkata. Outside of my academic work, I am
formally trained and passionate about Indian classical dance.
